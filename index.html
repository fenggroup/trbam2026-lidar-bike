Bicycling in the U.S.

  • Bicycling is underutilized with 0.5% of commuters riding a bicycle (vs. 69.2% commuters driving alone).1
  • 1,166 bicyclists were killed and 49,989 injured in 2023.2
  • Drivers overtaking bicyclists is one of the most dangerous encounters with rear-end collisions accounting for 40% bicyclist fatalities.3
  • Previous studies on overtaking bicyclists typically used lower resolution sensors (e.g., ultrasonic sensors4,5 or 2D lidar6), offering limited insights.

High resolution 3D lidar

  • Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging) is a laser-based sensing technology that captures detailed 3D representations of surroundings.
  • Lidar enables detailed 3D analysis of vehicle overtaking behavior, capturing precise distances and trajectories that traditional methods miss.
  • This study leverages lidar to bridge the gap between technological innovation and safer bicycling environments.

Study objectives

  • Collecting and sharing real-word bicycle riding data using high resolution lidar and cameras.
  • Develop an algorithm to automatically detect and track overtaking vehicles based on the lidar point clouds.
  • Develop quantitative surrogate safety measures for bicycling safety research.

Preliminary data collection

  • Two bicycle rides in Ann Arbor, MI
  • 4-lane arterial road with 35 mph speed limit
  • A painted bike lane on each side
  • Bicycle riding speed: 12-15 mph
  • Bicycle riding in the middle of the bike lane
  • 306 vehicle overtaking events

Surrogate safety measures

  • Passing distance: the closest distance from the lidar to the car within the passing region
  • Passing duration: the duration of time that a vehicle spends in the bicycle’s passing region
  • Vehicle Speed: estimated based on
    • bicycle speed estimated using frame-to-frame registration with ICP & RANSAC algorithms
    • relative vehicle speed estimated using numerical differentiation of vehicle centroid locations across frames

Results

  • 306 overtaking events from two trips
  • Passing distance validation over a sample of 98 events
    • MAE: 3.9 cm
    • MAPE: 2.5%

Discussions

  • Lane geometry strongly influenced clearance: left-lane ≈ 4.47 m, right-lane ≈ 1.86 m.
  • Close passes (<1.5 m) occurred at both low and high speeds, showing risk is not speed-dependent.
  • Passing duration revealed exposure over time; longer overtakes often due to congestion or intersections.
  • Safe overtaking depends on geometry + speed + traffic context, not any single factor.

Future work

  • Expand data collection across more routes, roadway types, and traffic conditions to improve generalizability.
  • Refine methodology with stronger ground-filtering and segmentation to improve vehicle detection reliability.
  • Analyze vehicle trajectories to better characterize lateral overtaking behavior across diverse environments.
